Cooking Tips:

Preheat Your Pan: Always preheat your pan before adding ingredients to ensure even cooking.

Taste as You Go: Taste your dish as you cook and adjust seasoning as needed to achieve the desired flavor.

Pat Meat Dry: Patting meat dry before cooking helps it to brown better and prevents steaming.

Rest Meat After Cooking: Let meat rest for a few minutes after cooking to allow the juices to redistribute, resulting in juicier meat.

Use Fresh Herbs: Fresh herbs add brightness and flavor to dishes. Add them towards the end of cooking for maximum impact.

Balance Flavors: Balance flavors by incorporating sweet, salty, sour, and bitter elements in your dish.

Read the Recipe: Read the entire recipe before you start cooking to ensure you have all the ingredients and understand the steps.

Don't Crowd the Pan: Overcrowding the pan can cause ingredients to steam instead of brown. Cook in batches if necessary.

Mise en Place: Prepare all your ingredients before you start cooking (mise en place) to make the cooking process smoother.

Kitchen Hacks:

Store Fresh Herbs in Water: Store fresh herbs like flowers in a glass of water in the fridge to keep them fresh longer.

Freeze Leftover Ingredients: Freeze leftover herbs, sauces, and stocks in ice cube trays for easy use in future recipes.

Keep Potatoes from Sprouting: Store potatoes with an apple to prevent them from sprouting.

Revive Stale Bread: Spritz stale bread with water and heat it in the oven to revive its freshness.

Use Citrus Zest: Use a microplane to zest citrus fruits and add bright flavor to dishes without adding extra liquid.

Keep Bananas Fresh: Wrap the stem of bananas in plastic wrap to extend their freshness.

Use Kitchen Shears: Kitchen shears are versatile tools for cutting herbs, trimming meat, and opening packages.

Prevent Boil-Overs: Place a wooden spoon across the top of a pot to prevent liquids from boiling over.

Use a Damp Towel Under Cutting Board: Place a damp towel under your cutting board to keep it from slipping while you chop.
